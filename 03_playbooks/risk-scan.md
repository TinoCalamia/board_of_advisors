# Risk Scan — How It Works

## When to Use

Use `/risk-scan` when you're about to commit to a specific initiative and want to stress-test it from every angle. Unlike other session types, risk scans are purely negative — advisors are told to find dangers, not opportunities.

Good risk scan topics:
- "Hiring a remote sales team"
- "Raising a Series A"
- "Entering the US market"
- "Switching from freemium to paid-only"
- "Partnering with a larger company"

## The Flow

```
1. You name a topic
   |
2. Context-loader gathers relevant data
   |
3. All advisors focus ONLY on risks (no opportunities)
   |
4. Synthesis produces a risk matrix with severity and likelihood ratings
```

## What You Get

- **Risk Matrix**: Table with severity, likelihood, source advisor, and mitigation for each risk
- **Top 3 Consensus Risks**: Dangers that multiple advisors independently identified
- **Contrarian Risk**: A risk only one advisor saw but that deserves attention
- **Prerequisites for Success**: Conditions that MUST be true for the initiative to work
- **Go/No-Go Recommendation**: Based on the overall risk profile

## Tips

- Run a risk scan BEFORE committing, not after
- The "Prerequisites for Success" section is often more actionable than the risks themselves
- A contrarian risk from a single advisor has historically been the most valuable insight
- Follow up with `/ask {advisor}` if a specific risk needs deeper exploration
