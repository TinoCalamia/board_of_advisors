# Board Debate — How It Works

## When to Use

Use `/debate` when you're facing a decision where smart people disagree and you want to see the arguments play out. Unlike `/convene-board` (single round), a debate has two rounds: initial positions, then rebuttals where advisors respond to each other.

Good debate topics:
- "Should we build our own AI model or use APIs?"
- "Should we acquire this competitor or compete against them?"
- "Remote-first or return to office?"

## The Flow

```
1. You pose a question
   |
2. Round 1: All advisors take initial positions (parallel)
   |
3. Fault lines identified: 2-3 biggest disagreements
   |
4. Round 2: Advisors rebut each other's arguments
   |
5. Synthesis highlights where positions shifted or hardened
```

## What Makes It Different

- **Two rounds** instead of one — advisors respond to each other, not just to the question
- **Fault lines** are explicitly identified before Round 2
- **Position shifts** are tracked — if an advisor concedes a point, that's significant
- **Unresolved tensions** are called out — these are where your judgment is most needed

## Tips

- The best debates come from questions where you genuinely don't know the answer
- Pay attention to which advisors change their position — that usually signals a strong argument
- Unresolved fault lines are not failures — they show you where the real tradeoffs are
